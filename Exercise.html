Grade 7 - Force

Review the definition of force, Newton’s Laws, the force formula, and try interactive PhET simulations.

Force is a push or pull acting on an object caused by interaction with another object. It can change the speed, direction, or shape of an object.
First Law: Objects remain at rest or motion unless acted upon by force.

Second Law: Acceleration depends on force and mass.

Third Law: Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.
F = m × a

F = Force
m = Mass
a = Acceleration

Grade 8 - Thermal Expansion

Understand why materials expand when heated, learn the basic formula, do quizziz and use PhET simulations to visualize particles and gases.

Thermal expansion is the increase in size of a material when its temperature rises. Most solids, liquids, and gases expand when heated.
When temperature increases, particles gain kinetic energy and vibrate or move faster. As a result, the particles spread farther apart, causing the material to expand.
ΔL = αL₀ΔT

ΔL = change in length
α = coefficient of linear expansion
L₀ = original length
ΔT = temperature change

Grade 9 - Magnetism & Dynamic Electricity

Explore magnetism, current, voltage, resistance, circuits, and interactive PhET simulations for Grade 9.

Dynamic electricity is electricity that occurs when electric charges move through a material. In most circuits, the moving charges are electrons traveling through wires.

Magnetism is a force produced by moving electric charges and magnetic materials. Magnets have north and south poles and create magnetic fields around them.
V = I × R

V = Voltage
I = Current
R = Resistance

Ohm’s Law shows the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance.
A circuit is a complete path through which electricity can flow.

Series circuit: one path for current.
Parallel circuit: multiple paths for current.

Resistance affects how easily current can move through a wire.
